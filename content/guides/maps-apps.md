## The 10-second pick

Use Apple Maps on iPhone and Amap on Android. Keep an offline backup even if your main app works.

If you are coming from Google Maps, read [Google Maps in China](/connect/google-maps/) first. The key issue is that access and accuracy are separate: an international eSIM can restore Google Maps access, but it does not fix the mainland China coordinate offset.

Start at the [connectivity hub](/connect/) for the wider phone setup, and keep the [eSIM guide](/connect/esim/) on your list before departure.

## Apple Maps

Apple Maps is the low-friction choice for iPhone travelers in China. It uses AutoNavi/Amap licensed data, has GCJ-02 built in, and is locally usable without a VPN.

The tradeoff is depth. English POI coverage is good enough for many tourist searches, and transit routes are usable, but small local shops and review depth are weaker than in a local China app.

## Amap

Amap is the local power tool. It added an official English version in 2025-01, now supports 15 languages, and has the strongest mix of POI, transit, and ride-hailing integration.

To switch it to English, use Me > Settings > General > Language > English, then restart the app.

Its weakness for visitors is friction: some deeper pages can still be in Chinese, and you should not rely on Amap as your only plan until you have tested registration on your own phone number.
