## Install before you fly

Install the eSIM before you fly to China. That is the one rule that matters more than brand choice.

International eSIM purchase and installation pages can be blocked inside China. If you wait until after landing, the plan you wanted may be unreachable at the exact moment you need data.

If you miss the window, use the fallback route: airport physical SIM options or hotel WiFi. For the tradeoff, read [eSIM vs local SIM for China](/connect/esim-vs-sim/).

## How international eSIMs work in China

An international eSIM does not work like a normal local China SIM.

The travel eSIM is installed on your phone before arrival, does not require ID registration, and routes data through an overseas gateway such as Singapore or Hong Kong. That overseas routing is why Google, WhatsApp, and Maps can work directly without a separate VPN.

That does not make every map app accurate. If you plan to use Google Maps, read [Google Maps in China](/connect/google-maps/) because access and map accuracy are separate problems.

## But I heard China supports eSIM now

In October 2025, China's three major domestic carriers opened phone eSIM support. That does not make domestic eSIM a practical tourist option.

Domestic carrier eSIM activation requires a Chinese ID and offline handling. For visitors, the useful split is still simple: install an international eSIM before arrival, or buy a physical SIM after landing.

China's domestic carriers do not sell tourist eSIMs. Airports sell physical SIMs, not the international travel eSIM you should have installed before departure.

## How to choose without chasing stale prices

Do not pick from a stale price table. China eSIM prices change fast, and this guide is not quoting fixed amounts.

Use a choice framework instead:

| Decision point | What to check |
| --- | --- |
| Data amount | Match the plan size to your trip length and daily map use. |
| Days covered | Check that validity covers your arrival day through departure day. |
| Hotspot sharing | Confirm tethering if you plan to share data with a laptop or travel partner. |
| Routing exit | Look for the overseas routing note, because that is what makes Google, WhatsApp, and Maps usable. |
| 5G support | Treat 5G as a bonus, not the core reason to buy. |
| Refund policy | Prefer a policy you understand before installation. |

Common options travelers compare include Nomad, Airalo, Holafly, and Jetpac. Use a live comparison site such as esimdb.com for current plan sizes and price comparison, then buy directly from the provider you choose.

## Setup checklist

- Confirm your phone is unlocked and supports eSIM.
- Buy the plan before departure.
- Install the eSIM before you fly.
- Land in China and turn on roaming data for that eSIM.
- Keep the [connectivity hub](/connect/) open for maps and app setup.
