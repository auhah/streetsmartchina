## Before you start

You need a mobile number that can receive international SMS, your passport available as a backup, and one Visa, Mastercard, American Express, JCB, Discover, or Diners card.

You do not need a Chinese bank account or a Chinese phone number. If you want the broader payment plan first, start at [how to pay in China](/money/).

## Limits and fees

The numbers below are verified as of 2026-07-04 from Alipay+ official guidance and the Wise limits table.

| Setup state | Transaction limit | Annual limit | Service fee |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Not passport verified | $500 per transaction | $2,000 per year | No service fee at <=¥200; 3% above ¥200. |
| Passport verified | $5,000 per transaction | $50,000 per year | No service fee at <=¥200; 3% above ¥200. |

Your card issuer may also add a 1-3% foreign-exchange fee.

## What you cannot do

Foreign-card users cannot use P2P transfers, red packets, or some small personal QR codes. For those cases, use merchant QR payment when available or pay cash.

For a second wallet, keep [WeChat Pay](/money/wechat-pay/) in your plan. For fallback rules, read [cash in China](/money/cash/). For ride-hailing payments, keep [DiDi in China](/didi-in-china/) on your list.
