## Short answer

Do not make Apple Pay your China payment plan.

Inside mainland China, Apple Pay at domestic POS terminals runs through the UnionPay channel. A foreign Visa or Mastercard sitting in Apple Wallet will not work at the vast majority of Chinese terminals.

Use [Alipay](/money/alipay/) as the main setup, add [WeChat Pay](/money/wechat-pay/) as the second wallet, and keep the [money hub](/money/) open for the full payment stack.

## The transit-card trap

The easy mistake is assuming Apple Wallet transit cards work like they do in many other countries.

For mainland China transit cards in Apple Wallet, the cited Apple Support pages for adding a card, riding with Express Transit, and fixing cards that cannot be added all point to the same practical requirement: you need a China UnionPay card already added to Wallet.

For most visitors, that makes Apple Wallet transit cards a bad plan. Use Alipay or WeChat ride codes instead, then keep [China train planning](/trains/) separate from your city-metro payment setup.

## Where it can work

Apple Pay can work only where a direct foreign-card payment would already have a realistic chance.

That means internationalized terminals: high-end hotels, airport duty-free, some airport lounge contexts, and some international chains. It does not mean local restaurants, street stalls, neighborhood shops, or taxi terminals.

Before relying on any direct card route, read the [foreign cards acceptance map](/money/cards/).

## Shanghai Metro exception

Shanghai Metro is the important exception to know, but keep it narrow.

From 2025-06, Shanghai Metro opened contactless foreign-card tap-to-pay for Visa, Mastercard, American Express, and JCB across its full 500+ station network. The verified fact is physical contactless bank-card tap-to-pay.

## The 2026 Apple Pay context

In 2026-01, Apple Pay for China-issued cards gained the ability to use the Visa network abroad, ending the earlier UnionPay-only setup for that outbound use case.

That is not the same problem a foreign tourist has inside mainland China. For inbound travel, the practical answer stays the same: Apple Pay is not your main payment method.
