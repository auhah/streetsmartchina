## Alipay first, WeChat second

Set up [Alipay](/money/alipay/) first, then add WeChat Pay as your second wallet. The reason is practical: small shops and individual merchants more often use WeChat codes, so dual wallet coverage gives you a better fallback than relying on one app.

For the wider payment plan, start at [how to pay in China](/money/). Keep [cash in China](/money/cash/) ready for failures, and use the [connectivity guides](/connect/) before downloading or setting up apps.

## Fees

The Beijing Municipal Government sources dated 2025-05-28 and 2026-03-26 are the cited official basis for the fee rules below.

| Rule | What it means |
| --- | --- |
| Permanent fee waiver | Single payments at or below ¥200 avoid the 3% service fee. |
| New-user 60-day window | From the first international-card binding, transactions up to ¥1,000 per day avoid the service fee for 60 days, with up to ¥30 saved on a single transaction. This is active as of Jul 2026. |
| Supported card organizations | Visa, MC, Amex, Discover, JCB, Diners, and UnionPay. |

## Limits

WeChat Pay uses RMB limits. That is different from the [Alipay setup guide](/money/alipay/), where the limits are shown in USD.

| Limit type | WeChat Pay tourist limit |
| --- | --- |
| Per transaction | ¥6,500 |
| Per month | ¥50,000 |
| Per year | ¥65,000 |

For a short-term tourist, these limits are usually enough for routine travel spending.

## What WeChat Pay is not for

Use WeChat Pay as a merchant-payment wallet, not as a way to send money to people. Personal QR payments, P2P-style routes, and group red packets can fail for the same practical reason foreign-card Alipay users hit P2P limits.
